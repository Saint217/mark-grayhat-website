QUESTIONS

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about cybersecurity services and ethical hacking

What is ethical hacking and how does it differ from malicious hacking?

Ethical hacking, also known as penetration testing, involves authorized attempts to bypass system security to identify potential data breaches and threats. Unlike malicious hackers, ethical hackers have explicit permission from the organization, work within legal boundaries, document findings, and help strengthen security rather than exploit vulnerabilities.

How often should my business conduct penetration testing?

The frequency of penetration testing depends on your industry, regulatory requirements, and risk profile. Generally, organizations should conduct penetration testing at least annually. However, testing should also be performed after significant infrastructure changes, new application deployments, or security incidents. High-risk industries like finance and healthcare may require quarterly assessments.

What's included in a security audit?

A comprehensive security audit includes: evaluation of network infrastructure, review of security policies and procedures, assessment of access controls, examination of data protection measures, compliance verification (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, etc.), vulnerability scanning, security awareness evaluation, incident response plan review, and detailed reporting with prioritized recommendations.

How long does a typical penetration test take?

The duration varies based on scope and complexity. A basic web application penetration test typically takes 1-2 weeks. Comprehensive network penetration testing for a medium-sized organization may require 2-4 weeks. Large enterprise assessments can take 4-8 weeks or longer. The timeline includes planning, active testing, analysis, and detailed reporting phases.

Will penetration testing disrupt my business operations?

Professional penetration testing is designed to minimize disruption. Testing can be scheduled during off-peak hours or maintenance windows. I work closely with your team to establish rules of engagement, define testing boundaries, and ensure business continuity. Any potential service disruptions are discussed and approved in advance.

What certifications and qualifications do you hold?

I hold multiple industry-recognized certifications including Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH), Offensive Security Certified Professional (OSCP), Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP), and CompTIA Security+. With 15 years of hands-on experience in cybersecurity, I've conducted over 500 security assessments across various industries.

What happens if vulnerabilities are discovered?

When vulnerabilities are identified, you receive a detailed report categorizing findings by severity (Critical, High, Medium, Low). Each vulnerability includes technical details, potential impact, proof-of-concept (where applicable), and specific remediation recommendations. I provide ongoing support during the remediation process and can perform retesting to verify fixes.

Do you offer ongoing security monitoring services?

Yes, I offer continuous vulnerability monitoring and periodic security assessments through retainer agreements. This includes automated vulnerability scanning, threat intelligence updates, security patch management guidance, quarterly penetration testing, and 24/7 incident response support. Continuous monitoring helps maintain a proactive security posture.
